Airport Analysis Dashboard: Executive Summary
The provided dashboard offers a high-level analysis of operations at San Francisco International Airport (SFO), focusing on flight routes, counts, and distances for March 2020.
The dashboard is organized into four key sections, providing insights into:
Longest Routes: Identifies the destinations with the maximum flight distances from SFO.
Day Wise Flight Count: Tracks the daily volume of flights over a specific period in March 2020.
Nearest Routes: Details the closest destinations and associated flight counts.
Flight Count (Overall): Provides a general overview of flight frequencies to various destinations.
Key Insights by Section
Longest Route Analysis:
The longest routes from SFO are to international destinations such as BLR (Bangalore), SIN (Singapore), DXB (Dubai), and MEL (Melbourne).
Flight counts for these long-haul routes generally range between 1K and 3K flights, with BLR and SIN having slightly higher counts.
Day Wise Flight Count (March 2020):
Flight counts remained relatively stable from March 4th to March 14th, hovering around 3.0K to 3.2K flights per day.
A noticeable decline in flight volume began around March 19th, dropping significantly to approximately 2.7K flights by March 24th and further decreasing towards the end of the month.
Nearest Route Analysis:
The nearest destinations are domestic airports, including MNH, RNO, and SBP.
Distances are significantly shorter, generally less than 350 miles.
The flight counts for these nearest routes vary widely, with MNH showing the highest count at 17,153 flights.
Flight Count (Overall):
LAX (Los Angeles) is the destination with the highest overall flight count from SFO, with 7,968 flights.
Other high-volume domestic destinations include JFK, SEA, LAS, ORD, SAN, POX, EWR, SLC, and BOS.
Flight counts for these routes range from approximately 2.2K to 8K.
